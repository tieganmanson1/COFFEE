Coffee Drinks

Espresso

An espresso is usually the strogest type of coffee, and is made by forcing boiling water through finely ground coffee beans. There is also a crema on top, which is produced by the emulsifation of oils in the coffee to produce a colloid. An espresso is served in a small cup and drank without milk. It is usually the base for most other coffee. The country of origin is Italy.

Americano

An americano is made by diluting a shot of espresso, the strength of an americano can be altered by adding more shots or adding more water. Many people add milk to an americano or you can drink it black.

Latte

A latte is also started using two shots of espresso, then adding steamed milk and foam.

Cappucino

A cappucino is created to be a third espresso, a third milk and a third foam. This makes it stonger than a latte but with more foam.
